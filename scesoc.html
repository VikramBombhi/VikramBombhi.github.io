Systems and Computer Engineering Society

What We Do

SCE Soc is the Carleton Systems and Computer Engineering Society. We are the society for everybody in the Faculty of Systems and Computer Engineering (SCE) which includes the Biomedical and Electrical, Communications, Computer Systems, and Software Engineering streams.
The story of the initial push of SCE Soc is kind of funny. A large group of first years in the Software and Computer Systems Engineering streams were looking for SCE Soc during the first year conference. When they found out that it had been defunct for a few years, they berated the first years they knew on how they would be able to start their own society. After struggling to create the society, a few of the upper years were able to swoop in and save the day. As a group, we found it jarring that there was no specialized society for our faculty streams. We technically fall under IEEE but that was focused more towards the Faculty of Electronics rather our own faculty.

What SCESOC is like, and what we focus on

The new SCE Soc will be very similar to the old SCE Soc where we want a society that will encourage both social and academic growth of our members. There is only one real difference between the new and old SCE Soc. The old SCE Soc stood for Software and Computer Engineering Society and they concentrated more on the Software and Computer System Engineering streams. The new SCE Soc will be facilitating to the entire SCE faculty.
Like the old SCE Soc, we will be organizing social and academic events like study groups, workshops, help sessions from upper years and professors/TAs as well as social events. As this is our first year and we are a small society at the moment, we are going to focus on more social events to get more interest and participation in the society. In the future, we will want to organize things like conferences, networking events. We will are also interested in starting a test library and a collection of internship/co-op contacts that our members will be able to utilize.
