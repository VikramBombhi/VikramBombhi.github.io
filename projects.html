Steganography

Steganography is "the practice of concealing messages or information within other nonsecret text or data". After watching this video (on the left) I was inspired to create my own adaptation of the idea with which I could hide text within an image. My method relies on the idea of the "least significant bit". By finding the RGB value for a pixel in and converting it to binary we can change the last bit. The last bit is the least significant bit and so has the least amount of influence on the outcome of the RGB value so after the pixel been altered the difference in the pixel is impossible to detect with the human eye.



EngComp 2015

I competed in the software engineering portion of the engineering competition held at Carleton University. The groups consisted of four people tasked to solve a situation where we must determine if a washer is defective or not within 7 hours. Armed with a set of "good" washers that the manufacturer provided we had to determine if a washer was defective using only an image of the washer in question. After analyzing multiple washers the results had to be outputted as a CSV(comma separated value). My group chose to determine of the washer in question was defective by comparing its surface area to that of a washer from the "good" batch. The flow chart outlines the conceptual idea behind our algorithm when determining whether or not the washer was defective. The presentation slides for the project can be found here and the full repo can be found here . Our algorithm was 62% accurate for the set of images giving at the competition



Carleton Planetary Robotics Team

The Carleton Planetary Robotics Team builds and competes in the University Rover Challenge Competition. This is the first year Carleton is participating in the competition so most, if not all, of the software is being built from the ground up. I have been working in the global positioning system and user interface teams. I completed all preliminary research for the positioning system which included presenting my findings to the team. The user interface team consists of me and two others who have been asked to create a in browser user interface for the rover using javascript and python.



SCESOC (Systems and Computer Engineering Society)

Not all software engineering is done using a keyboard and mouse. Many problems and solutions can be found with the help of others. I myself was looking for a society at Carleton that would allow me to talk to fellow software engineers and expand my network. Almost every engineering department has a society at Carleton that brings them networking nights and support in their programs however I was amazed to find out that the Systems and Computer Engineering department had no such society. Talking to fellow students in my department it was clear that everyone wanted one so I decided to start one on my own, or rather restart the old one that had died out years ago. The new society is now fully operational with plenty of interest. The image on the left is the executives of the society on the first day of 'official' work.
