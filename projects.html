The 2015 Carleton Engineering Competition

I competed in the software engineering portion of the engineering competition held at Carleton University. The groups consisted of four people tasked to solve a situation where we must determine if a washer is defective or not within 7 hours. Armed with a set of “good” washers that the manufacturer provided we had to determine if a washer was defective using only an image of the washer in question. After analyzing multiple washers the results had to be outputted as a CSV(comma separated value). My group chose to determine of the washer in question was defective by comparing its surface area to that of a washer from the “good” batch. The flow chart outlines the conceptual idea behind our algorithm when determining whether or not the washer was defective. The presentation slides for the project can be found here.
